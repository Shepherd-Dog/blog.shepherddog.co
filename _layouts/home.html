What Are You Great At?

Is it caring for the elderly? Building smart devices that work with an app? Providing professional education? Roasting coffee?

We're great at building and maintaining software tools for businesses — tools used by your customers and employees. We listen to your needs and figure out how to make the technology work for you, whether that means owning every bit of the process or collaborating with your internal team to fill gaps. We've worked with businesses across many industries and have the skills to rapidly absorb new information so we can hit the ground running.

We handle the tools, systems, and processes needed to make great software so you don't have to.

Our Strengths

It's hard to boil a company (even a small company like ours) down into three bullets but this layout looks good and we don't want to overwhelm you. In addition to these, you can read about our core values or check out some of our favorite blog posts.

  • Focus

    We're a small, focused team. Our size has allowed us to streamline our communication and project management processes. Even though we're small, our track record proves that we can handle everything needed to build and maintain software from initial discovery to final testing.

    This means that what we build will be true to what your business needs.

  • Flexibility

    We are technology-neutral, know many programming languages, and have experience with a variety of frameworks.

    This means we will choose the right tools for your new project based upon your business needs. Or if you have existing tools in place we can quickly jump in and start making an impact.

  • Fdedication

    We have an unfair advantage — we care more. We don't want to put crap into the world. We know that what we build is a reflection of ourselves and that what we build for your business is a reflection of you. We pour ourselves entirely into a project and treat it as if it were our own.

    This means that we will deliver what we say we will no matter how complicated and difficult the problem is.
